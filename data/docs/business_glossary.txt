BUSINESS GLOSSARY
=================

Revenue / Sales / Total Sales:
  All three terms refer to the SUM of the total column in the orders table.
  SQL: SUM(orders.total)
  Do not confuse with product price — total already accounts for quantity.

Return Rate:
  The percentage of orders that were returned.
  Calculated as: COUNT(returns.id) / COUNT(orders.id) * 100
  Usually grouped by product category or region.

Low Stock:
  Products where the stock column in the products table is less than 10.
  SQL condition: WHERE products.stock < 10

Top Customers:
  Customers ranked by their total spending across all orders.
  SQL: GROUP BY customers.id ORDER BY SUM(orders.total) DESC

Weekend Orders:
  Orders placed on Saturday or Sunday.
  SQLite: strftime('%w', order_date) IN ('0', '6')
  where '0' = Sunday, '6' = Saturday

Weekday Orders:
  Orders placed Monday through Friday.
  SQLite: strftime('%w', order_date) NOT IN ('0', '6')

Last Year:
  Orders where order_date falls within the previous calendar year.
  SQLite: strftime('%Y', order_date) = strftime('%Y', 'now', '-1 year')

This Quarter / Current Quarter:
  Orders in the current 3-month period based on today's date.
  SQLite: strftime('%Y-%m', order_date) between quarter start and end months.

Monthly Revenue Trend:
  Revenue grouped by month. Use strftime('%Y-%m', order_date) to group.

Average Order Value (AOV):
  AVG(orders.total) — the mean transaction amount.

Customer Segment:
  The segment column classifies customers into Consumer, Corporate, or Home Office.
  Corporate customers typically have higher AOV than Consumer.

Product Category Performance:
  Compare SUM(orders.total) grouped by products.category with a JOIN to orders.

Stock Level Analysis:
  Query the products table directly. No join needed unless combining with sales data.
